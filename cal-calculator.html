The basics

What is a CAL?

Think of it like a restaurant. You (the BDM) take the order. Onboarding cooks it. A CAL — Client Access Licence — is how many seats the customer needs at the table. Get the count wrong and the kitchen prepares the wrong amount of food.

A CAL is a licence permitting a device, user, or system connection to access the Bepoz platform. All licences are held and counted at Head Office. Remote venues connect to HO via VPN through a SmartController — there is always exactly one SmartController per venue, and that connection is what's licenced.

User CAL vs Device CAL

Two types of CAL for remote access

User CAL Recommended

One licence per person, regardless of how many devices they connect from. A manager logging in from their laptop at home and their desktop at the office is still just 1 CAL.

Best for: roaming staff, managers, HO users who move between devices
3 area managers with remote access = 3 User CALs

Device CAL

One licence per device, regardless of how many users share it. Five staff sharing one back-office PC on a roster is still just 1 CAL.

Best for: shared fixed terminals with many users taking turns on one machine
2 shared manager PCs = 2 Device CALs, no matter how many staff use them
For most hospitality groups, User CALs are the right call. Managers and HO staff typically roam across devices. Device CALs only make sense when a single fixed terminal is shared by many users.
